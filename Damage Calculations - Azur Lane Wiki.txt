This page goes over all the formulas and terms used when calculating the
final damage dealt by attacking ships, and the damage received by target
ships, in Azur Lane.

This wiki page is very confusing, due to how complicated the code is and
our ability to clearly explain everything. If there are any errors, feel
free to give us feedback!


*Notes:*

  * Unless otherwise stated, assume that *all decimal values are
    floored* to the nearest whole number.
  * This guide is written from the perspective of *"Attacker"* ships
    dealing damage and *"Target"* ships receiving damage. Unless
    otherwise stated, Enemy ships or Allied ships can be the attacker or
    target.
      o *"Enemy"* and *"Allied"* are used when the effect applies to
        NPC / Player ships *only*.


    Damage Types

Most damage dealt scales with the attacker's offensive stats:

  * *Gun (or Shelling)* - damage dealt by shells/bullets from ship Main
    Guns and Secondary Guns; scales with FirepowerFP (Firepower).
  * *Torpedo* - damage dealt by ship Torpedos; scales with TorpedoTRP
    (Torpedo).
  * *Anti-Air* - damage dealt by ship Anti-Air mechanics <#Anti-
    Air_Guns>; scales with Anti-airAA (Anti-Air).
  * *Aircraft* - damage dealt by aircraft launched from ships; scales
    with AviationAVI (Aviation).
      o Includes all aircraft armaments except ASW (ie. aircraft
        Cannons/Torpedos *still scale with AVI* rather than FP/TRP/AA).
  * *Anti-Submarine Warfare* - damage dealt by ASW mechanics <#Anti-
    Submarine_Warfare>; scales with Anti-submarine warfareASW (Anti-
    Submarine Warfare).

Damage without conventional stat scaling includes:

  * *Burn Damage* - a damage over time (DoT) effect.
  * *Flood Damage* - a damage over time (DoT) effect.
  * *Ramming Damage* - dealt when ships collide with one another.
  * *Collision Damage* - dealt when enemy ships explode upon contact
    with your fleet.

Barrages are composed of one of more damage types, and may deal fixed
damage or have unique stat scaling.

  * See this wiki page <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Barrage> for
    specific data on individual barrages.


    Projectile Types

There are five main projectiles fired by ships, from either their
equipped weapons and/or barrages.

  * *Arcing Shells* - These are shells that travel in an arcing pattern.
    These shells are also able to go over shields. BB Guns and CB Guns
    fall into this category.
  * *Straight Shells* - These are shells that travel in a straight line.
    These are blocked by shields. DD Guns, CL Guns, and CA Guns fall
    into this category.
  * *Torpedoes* - These travel underwater, and cannot be blocked outside
    of specific shields that reference Torpedoes. In addition,
    Airstrikes can clear all torpedoes currently traveling.
  * *AA Bullets* - These only do damage to aircraft. AA Guns fall into
    this category.
  * *Plane Ordnances* - These are the bombs/rockets/torpedoes that drop
    from planes during an airstrike. The bombs equipped by aircraft fall
    into this category.


    Ammo Types

There are four main types of ammunition for non-aircraft weaponry and
barrages:

  * *Normal (Yellow)* - These are regular rounds that disappear on
    impact with the first target hit, or the top or bottom boundaries.
    This is the most common shell type used by target ships.
  * *High Explosive (Red)* - /Also known as HE./ These rounds are
    similar to Normal shells, but have a chance to set target ships on
    fire in exchange for slower velocity.
  * *Armor-Piercing (Blue)* - /Also known as AP./ These rounds are
    similar to Normal shells, but have faster shell velocity and can
    pierce through their targets. Usually AP weapons can pierce once (up
    to 2 targets hit per bullet).
  * *Semi-Armor-Piercing (Purple)* - /Also known as SAP./ These rounds
    are similar to AP shells, but with different Armor Modifiers.
    Currently, only the Twin 135mm <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Twin_135mm_(Model_1938)>, Twin 203mm (Model 1927) <https://
    azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Twin_203mm_(Model_1927)>, Triple 406mm
    (Model 1940 Prototype) <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Triple_406mm_(Model_1940_Prototype)> fire this type of shell. Note
    that the SAP shells fired by the Twin 135mm (Model 1938) do not
    pierce targets.

*Notes*:

  * Each ammo type has their own *ArmorModifier* associated with it.
    Refer to their respective equipment pages <https://
    azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Equipment_List> for those values.
  * Barrages can drastically modify the characteristics of Ammo Types,
    see the ship's specifc barrage information for more details.


    Armor Modifiers

There are three main armor types:

  * *Light Armor* - Armor type for most Destroyers (DDs) and Light
    Cruisers (CLs), with some exceptions.
  * *Medium Armor* - Armor type for most Heavy Cruisers (CAs), Aircraft
    Carriers (CVs), Light Aircraft Carriers (CVLs) and Battlecruisers
    (BCs), with some exceptions.
  * *Heavy Armor* - Armor type for most Battleships (BBs), with some
    exceptions.

To view the Armor type per ship, see the List of Ships by Stats
<https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/List_of_Ships_by_Stats> wiki page for
those values.


    Stat Modifiers

These are buffs that increase the *ScalingStat* of the damage.
Generally, all bullet damage scales off of Firepower *Firepower*, all
torpedo damage scales off of Torpedo *Torpedo*, all AA gun damage scales
off of Anti-air *Anti-Air*, and and all aircraft damage scales off of
Aviation *Aviation*, with some exceptions.

Notations ShipBaseStat 	The base value of a stat. This is ship dependent.
CatStatMultiplier 	Meowfficers <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
Meowfficer> can also give stat bonuses based on their 3 inherent stats
(Logistics, Directives, and Tactics). This total percent bonus is known
as CatStatMultiplier. See this wiki page <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/
wiki/Meowfficer#Stats> for more information.
StatPercentBuffs 	The *percentage* buff to a stat.
SkillFlatBuffs 	Some ships (ex. North Carolina <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/North_Carolina>, Ingraham <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Ingraham>) increase a ScalingStat by a
percentage of an additional stat. In addition, certain augment modules
increase stats by a non-percentage amount from their skills (ex.
Officer's Sword <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Officer%27s_Sword>).
These stat increases are treated seperately from everything else.


      Flat Stat Modifiers

These are the stat buffs that increase any *ScalingStat* by a non-
percentage amount. All sources are additive to each other, and to the
base stat.

Source include:

  * Equipment <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Equipment> equipped
    onto the ship.
  * Fleet Technology <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Fleet_Technology>
  * Meowfficer <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Meowfficer> _Talents_

Meowfficers <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Meowfficer> can also give
stat bonuses based on their 3 inherent stats (Logistics, Directives, and
Tactics). This total percent bonus is known as CatStatMultiplier. See
this wiki page <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Meowfficer#Stats> for
more information.

In the damage formula, the ScalingStat is divided by 100. Therefore, the
FlatScalingStat is:


FlatScalingStat=(ShipBaseStat×CatStatMultiplier)+∑i=1n(FlatStatBuffsi)


      Percentage Stat Modifiers

These are buffs that increase any *ScalingStat* by a percentage amount.
All sources of stat % increases stack additively.

Sources include:

  * Ship Skills (both on self and from other ships).
  * Equipment <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Equipment> Skills (both
    equipped onto the ship and from other ships).
  * Meowfficer <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Meowfficer> _Skills_
    (see this wiki page <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Meowfficer#List_of_Meowfficers_and_Skills> for more information).
  * Formation <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Combat#Formation> Stat
    Bonuses.

and more!

PercentStatModifier=1+∑i=1n(StatPercentBuffsi)


      Misc. Skill Flat Stat Buffs

These skill buffs that increase any ScalingStat by a flat amount.

Sources include:

  * Straight Buffs (ex. Zeppy's <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Zeppy> *Taste My Wrath!*).
  * Stat Conversion Skils (ex. North Carolina's <https://
    azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/North_Carolina> *AA Firepower*)
      o /Stat Conversions use the Base Stat + any Equipment Stats when
        calculating the conversion amount, NOT the CurrentStat./
  * Augment Module Skills (ex. Officer's Sword <https://
    azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Officer%27s_Sword>)


FlatStatModifier=∑i=1n(SkillFlatBuffsi)


      Final Effective Stat

There are 2 values that can be used in calculations:
*CurrentScalingStat* or *StatMultiplier*.

  * For all formulas with a stat value, assume the stat is calculated in
    the same way as *CurrentScalingStat* unless otherwise stated.


        CurrentStat

CurrentScalingStat=(FlatScalingStat×PercentStatModifier)+FlatStatModifier


CurrentScalingStat=[(ShipBaseStat×CatStatMultiplier)+∑i=1n(FlatStatBuffsi)]×(1+∑i=1n(StatPercentBuffsi))+∑i=1n(SkillFlatBuffsi)


        StatMultiplier

StatMultiplier=1+CurrentScalingStat100


StatMultiplier=1+(FlatScalingStat×PercentStatModifier)+FlatStatModifier100


    Universal Modifiers


      DamageRatioBullet

These are effects that affect a ship's *damage dealt* _without any
specification_ (not including Level Advantage <#Level_Advantage>).

Sources include:

  * Ship Skills (ex. Enterprise <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Enterprise>'s *Lucky E*).
  * Ammo Bonus <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Combat#Ammo>.
  * Meowfficers <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Meowfficer> (Flame's
    Aggression).
  * Exercise Modifiers <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Exercise#Special_Modifiers>.

and more!

TotalDRB=1+∑i=1n(UniversalDamageBuffsi)

For example, if Enterprise <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
Enterprise> triggered *Lucky E*, and the fleet was *Out-Of-Ammo*, then:

TotalDRB=1+(1+(−0.5))=1.5


      InjureRatio

These are effects applied *to a target*, that affect the target's damage
received _without any specification_ (not including Threat Level
<#Threat_Level>). These stack multiplicatively with one another. /
Negative values indicate damage reduction./

Sources include:

  * Ship Skills, for example:
      o Helena <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Helena>'s *Radar Scan*.
      o Roon µ <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Roon_%C2%B5>'s *Encore
        Flare*.
      o Any universal damage reduction (ex. Graf Zeppelin <https://
        azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Graf_Zeppelin>'s *Iron Blood Wings*).
  * Exercise Modifiers <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Exercise#Special_Modifiers>.

and more!

TotalInjureRatio=∏i=1n(1+Debuffi)


    Weapon Type Modifiers


      DamageRatioByType

These are effects that affect a ship's damage dealt _with a specific
type of weapon_.

Sources include:

  * Ship Skills, for example:
      o Kinu (Retrofit) <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Kinu>'s
        *Demon's Wish* (increases Torpedo TRP DMG dealt).
      o Atago <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Atago>'s *Arsonist*
        (increases HE DMG dealt).
  * Airspace Control / ACV <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Combat#Airspace_Control> (modifies AVI DMG dealt).


      InjureRatioByType

These are effects applied *to a target*, that affect the target's damage
received _by a specific type of weapon_. /Negative values indicate
damage reduction./

Sources include:

  * Ship skills, for example:
      o Tanikaze (Retrofit) <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
        Tanikaze>'s *Target Designator: Torpedo* (increases target
        Torpedo TRP DMG taken).
      o Valiant <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Valiant>'s *Leading
        the Way* (increases target Firepower Firepower DMG taken).
      o August von Parseval <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
        August_von_Parseval>'s *Hex-Principle of Shattering* (increases
        target Aviation Aviation DMG taken).
      o Mogami <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Mogami>'s *AP
        Protection* (decreases self AP DMG taken).
  * Armor Break <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Armor_breaking>
    (increases target Firepower Firepower DMG taken).


      Armor Break

*Armor Break* (also called *Armor Shatter*) is a debuff that increases
the Firepower Firepower scaling damage a target receives. Most Armor
Break debuffs can only be applied to *Heavy Armor* targets, which
include most Battleships <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
Category:Battleships> and some Battlecruisers <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Category:Battlecruisers>.

Armor Break with the same "BuffID" do not stack; instead, applying Armor
Break again will only refresh the pre-existing debuff's duration.
Multiple sources of Armor Break with /different/ BuffIDs /can/ stack.

DamageRatioByType and InjureRatioByType are additive with each other *if

    Critical Hits

These are hits that do significantly more damage than usual.


      Critical Rate

The Critical Rate for your ships is based on a formula, with the scaling
stats calculated in the same method as up *here
<#Final_Effective_Stat>*. The formula is as follows:


CriticalRate=0.05+(AttackerHitAttackerHit+TargetEvasion+2000)+(AttackerLuck−TargetLuck+LevelDifference5000)+∑i=1nCriticalRateBuffsn

CriticalRate≤1


      Critical Damage

Notations CriticalBit 	This value is *0* when the damage is not a
critical hit, and *1* when the damage is a critical hit.
CriticalDMGBuff 	Any Critical Damage buffs provided by ship skills and/
or equipment. All CriticalDMGBuffs stack additively with one another.

The total *CriticalModifier* is as follows:

CriticalModifier=1+CriticalBit×(0.5+∑i=1nCriticalDMGBuffn)


Example Scenario 1: Howe <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Howe>
equipped with a Type 1 AP Shell <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
Type_1_Armor_Piercing_Shell> lands a critical hit on a target.

CriticalModifier=1+1×(0.5+0.25)=1.75


    Aimed Shot (Manual) Modifiers

*ManualBonus* is a bonus given to BB Main Gun and DDGm Missile Damage if
it is manually aimed by the player.

*AimedShotBuffs* are modifiers that affect the damage dealt by the 1st
salvo, seperate from the ManualBonus. This modifier can still apply even
when not manually aiming.

The default manual bonus is *20%*. Be careful, since some skills *change
the default manual bonus* instead of increasing the damage dealt by
aimed shots. If the shot isn't manually aimed, then *ManualBonus* is set
to 0.


ManualModifier=1+ManualBonus+AimedShotBuffs


Example Scenario 1: A shot fired by Gascogne <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Gascogne> (*Precision Salvo Control*), and the
shot is manually aimed.

ManualModifier=1+0.4+0=1.4


Example Scenario 2: The first volley of a salvo fired by Jean Bart
<https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Jean_Bart> (*Pirate Soul*), and the
shot is manually aimed.

ManualModifier=1+0.6+0.6=2.2


Example Scenario 3: The first volley of a salvo fired by Jean Bart
<https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Jean_Bart> (*Pirate Soul*), and the
shot is NOT manually aimed.

ManualModifier=1+0+0.6=1.6


    Level Advantage

For Threat Level Mechanics, see this wiki page <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Combat#Threat_Level>.

These are the damage modifiers gained (or lost) due to the difference in
ship levels between allied and target ships, up to (± 50% damage dealt/
received).

Once a stage is considered *Threat Level 0 (Safe)*, the levels of
*enemy* ships are artifically decreased for the purposes of these
formulas. If you are calculating damage dealt by an enemy, allied ship
levels are not lowered. See this wiki page <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Combat#Threat_Level> for more information.


LevelDifference=AttackerShipLevel−(TargetShipLevel−ArtificalLevelDecrease)

LevelAdvDmgModifier=1+2×LevelDifference100

0.5≤LevelAdvDmgModifier≤1.5.


    Threat Level

For Threat Level Mechanics, see this wiki page <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Combat#Threat_Level>.

This is a damage reduction applied *only to allied ships*, based on the
threat level of the stage. See this wiki page <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Combat#Threat_Level> for more information.


ThreatLevelDmgModifier=1−[2×(MaxStageThreatLevel−CurrentStageThreatLevel)100]


On stages without Threat Level, *MaxStageThreatLevel* and
*CurrentStageThreatLevel* are both set to *0*.


Example Scenario 1: *MaxStageThreatLevel* = 10,
*CurrentStageThreatLevel* = 0.


ThreatLevelDmgModifier=1−2×(10−0)100=0.8


    Air Damage Reduction

For CV Detection Mechanics, see this wiki page <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Combat#Detection_Line>.

This is damage reduction for AVI damage gained from a *defending ship's
AA* stat, with the variables calculated in the same method as up here
<#Final_Effective_Stat>.

CloakBit=1 if the attacking CV is considered *"hidden"*, and *0* otherwise.


TargetAAModifier=150150+CurrentAA+(CloakBit×0.1)


TargetAAModifier≤1


    Final Damage Dealt

The following calculations account for every quantitative factor applied
to both the allied ship launching the attack, and the target ship
receiving the attack. In practice, you will likely not be able to land
all parts of the attack geometrically, see *here <#Weapon_Accuracy>* for
more details.

*Notes:*

  * The minimum damage dealt is always *1* if the damage isn't evaded.
  * /Barrages can have multiple different parts; calculate each part
    seperately then add them together to get a final damage number./


      Guns / Torpedoes

This is the damage dealt to the target *by 1 weapon bullet*. The
effective scaling stat is usually Firepower FP / Torpedo TRP
resepectively, unless otherwise stated. /This formula also works for
depth charges equipped by vanguard ships, with the scaling stat being
Anti-submarine warfare ASW./

  * The default *WeaponScalingCoefficient* is 1, however some barrages /
    equipment might change this value. See the weapon's page for more
    information.


WeaponStatMultiplier=1+CurrentScalingStat×WeaponScalingCoefficient100


DamageDealt=[WeaponBaseDmg×WeaponCoeff×SlotEfficiency×WeaponStatMultiplier+random(0,1,2)]×WeaponArmorModifier×TotalDRB×TotalInjureRatio×TotalDRT×TotalHunter×LevelAdvDmgModifier×ThreatLevelDmgModifier×ManualModifier×CriticalModifier


*Example Scenario*: Solo Level 120 Howe <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/
wiki/Howe> with Type 1 AP Shell <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
Type_1_Armor_Piercing_Shell> and Super Heavy Shell <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Super_Heavy_Shell> equipped, fires a +10
enhanced T3 Triple 406mm Mk 6 Main Gun Mount <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Triple_406mm_(16%22/45_Mk_6)#Type_3-0> at a
Level 125 Light Armor target affected by Helena <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Helena>'s *Radar Scan* on Threat Level 10;
Random = 0, with some Fleet Technology bonuses.

  * *WeaponBaseDmg* = 156, *WeaponCoeff* = 1.05, *SlotEfficiency* = 1.4
  * *WeaponStatMultiplier* = (419 + 67 + 85 + 25 + 5) * 1 / 100 x 1.12 +
    1 = 7.7312
      o *FlatStatBuffs* = 67 + 85 due to Auxiliary, +25 from Main Gun,
        +5 from Fleet Technology Bonus.
      o *PercentStatModifier* = 1 + 0.12 = 1.12 due to skills.
      o No *CatStatMultiplier*, so it was set to 1.
      o No *AugmentModuleSkillException*, no *StatConversionBuffs*, so
        those were set to 0
  * *WeaponArmorModifier* = 1.4
  * *TotalDRB* = 1
      o No universal damage buffs anywhere.
  * *TotalInjureRatio* = 1.4
      o Helena <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Helena> debuff is +40%
        damage taken by targets affected.
  * *TotalDRT* = 1
      o No specific weapon type buffs.
  * *TotalHunter* = 1
      o No hunter buffs.
  * *LevelAdvDmgModifier* = 1 + (2*(120-125)/100) = 0.9
      o 10% less damage due to levels.
  * *ThreatLevelDmgModifier* = 1
      o Doesn't apply to allied damage dealt.
  * *ManualModifier* = 1
      o Shot wasn't manually aimed.
  * *CriticalModifier* = 1
      o Shot wasn't a critical hit.

Therefore:

DamageDealt=[156×1.05×1.4×7.7312+0]×1.4×1×1.4×1×1×0.9×1×1≈3127.43

Remember, this is only for *ONE* bullet that did not critically hit.


      Aircraft Ordnance

This is the damage dealt to the target *by 1 aircraft bomb/torpedo/
rocket*. It is calculated similarly to Guns/Torpedoes, with a few key
differences:

  * The scaling stat is almost always Aviation AVI, unless otherwise
    stated. Plane AA guns use Aviation AVI for their calculations.
  * *Weapon* refers to a singular Aircraft bomb/torpedo/rocket. For
    total plane damage, calculate each bomb/torpedo/rocket type
    seperately, and add them together.
  * For Aircraft bombs/rockets, the default *WeaponScalingCoefficient*
    is 0.8, however all aircraft torpedoes and fighter/torpedo bomber
    rockets have a *WeaponScalingCoefficient* of 1.
  * All ships (both Allied and Enemies) have *TargetAAModifier*, which
    uses their AA stat to reduce incoming Aviation AVI damage.


WeaponStatMultiplier=1+CurrentScalingStat×WeaponScalingCoefficient100


DamageDealt=[WeaponBaseDmg×WeaponCoeff×SlotEfficiency×WeaponStatMultiplier+random(0,1,2)]×WeaponArmorModifier×TotalDRB×TotalInjureRatio×TotalDRT×TotalHunter×LevelAdvDmgModifier×ThreatLevelDmgModifier×CriticalModifier×TargetAAModifier


    Weapon Reload

Reload *Reload* is the rate at which weapons charge up. For rate of
fire, take the reciprocal of the calculated time interval. For example,
if 'fire rate' is 2s, rate of fire will be 0.5 waves/second.

Notations WeaponReloadTime 	The default ReloadTime of a weapon, in
seconds. (This Wiki lists this stat as /RLD/ in the weapon tables)
CurrentReload 	Reload stat of a ship calculated in the same method as up
here <#Final_Effective_Stat>.


      Guns / Torpedoes

The reload time of a gun or torpedo is affected by the Reload Reload
stat of your ship. The formula for this is as follows:

ReloadTime=WeaponReloadTime×200CurrentReload+100+VolleyTime+AbsoluteCooldown


*Notes:*

  * The weapon reload time displayed in-game does not consider
    *VolleyTime* nor *AbsoluteCooldown*.
  * To see the default WeaponReloadTime value, unequip the weapon and
    check its reload in the Depot <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Equipment#Equipment_(Standard)>.
  * *VolleyTime* and *AbsoluteCooldown* are both gun dependent.
      o *VolleyTime* is specific to the gun itself, and can be found on
        the specific gun's wiki page.
      o *AbsoluteCooldown* is theorized to be based on the gun type, and
        not the gun itself.


      Airstrike

The reload for airstrikes is similar to that of guns, except that it
considers the reload of every plane equipped to the ship. The formula
for this is as follows:

LaunchCooldown=∑iN(cooldown(Planei)×numberof(Planei))∑iNnumberof(Planei)×2.2×200CurrentReload+100+0.033

      AA Damage

See also: Talk:Combat <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Talk:Combat>
for more specific details

The damage per instance of Anti-Air fire is affected by the Anti-air AA
stat of *all ships with the same type of AA guns in the fleet*
(including Ghost AA Guns). The formula for this is as follows:

FinalDamage=∑foreachship(∑foreachgun(EquipmentDamage×SlotEfficiency×NumberofMounts)×AAMultiplier)

Each point of AA thus provides approximately a 1% bonus to AA damage.
This damage is then distributed among the target planes, though with
some random factor. Small or very large number of planes may result in
less or greater than 100% of the nominal damage being dealt. Average
results can be seen in this plot:

<https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/File:Mean_aa_damage_plot.png>

Refer to Talk:Combat <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Talk:Combat> for
the exact mechanics.


      AA Reload

The reload is dependent on the Reload Reload stat of *all ships with the
same type of AA guns in the fleet* (including Ghost AA Guns). This is
equal to the average reload time of all equipped AA guns in the fleet,
plus a constant.

IndividualAAReload=WeaponReloadTime×200CurrentReload+100


AAReload=∑i(IndividualAAReloadi)NumberOfAAGuns+AbsoluteCooldown


*Notes:*

  * The weapon reload time displayed in-game does not consider
    *AbsoluteCooldown*, nor average the reload with all other ships in
    the fleet.
      o *AbsoluteCooldown* for AA Guns is theorized to be 0.8667s.
  * To see the default WeaponReloadTime value, unequip the weapon and
    check its reload in the Depot <https://azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/
    Equipment#Equipment_(Standard)>.


      AA Range

The coverage of *traditional* AA fire is outlined by the dotted lines
surrounding your escort fleet. This is equal to the average range of all
equipped traditional AA guns in the fleet:

EffectiveRange=∑in(AAGunRangei)NumberOfAAGuns

Unlike traditional AA Guns, Time Fuze AA Gun range is semi-circular /
rectangular from the Main Fleet position of the ship equipping it. This
combined AA Gun is placed on a ship that is equipping a Time Fuze AA
Gun, with priority given to flagship, top, then bottom positions in the
Main Fleet. Time Fuze AA Gun range is calculated in the same method as
above.

    Collision Damage


      Ramming

This is damage dealt when an attacking unit physically collides with a
target unit. It is computed as follows:

RammingDamage=0.025×AttackerShipMaxHP×TargetShipMaxHP×(1+TargetShipRamAttackBonus)×(1−AttackerShipRamDefenseBonus)

RammingDamage≥0.05×AttackerShipMaxHP


      Enemy Surface Ship to Allied Main Fleet

This is damage when a target surface ship reaches the end of the screen
and deals damage to your Main Fleet.

*Notes*:

  * *CurrentEnemyHP* is a percentage, not the exact value.
  * In this formula, *CollisionDamage* is _floored_ to the nearest whole
    number.
  * All scaling stats calculated in the same method as up *here
    <#Final_Effective_Stat>*.

CollisionDamage=[(EnemyFP+EnemyTRP)×2.2+EnemyLevel−ArtificialLevelDecrease2]×TotalAlliedInjureRatio×(CurrentEnemyHP%×0.7+0.3)×(1+LevelDifference200)×ThreatLevelDmgModifier


CollisionDamage>=10. If this value goes below 10 for any reason, 10 is
used instead.


      Enemy Submarine to Allied Main Fleet

This is damage when a target submarine reaches the end of the screen and
deals damage to your Main Fleet.

*Notes*:

  * *CurrentEnemyHP* is a percentage, not the exact value.
  * In this formula, *CollisionDamage* is _floored_ to the nearest whole
    number.


CollisionDamage=[EnemyTRP×3.5+EnemyLevel−ArtificialLevelDecrease2]×TotalAlliedInjureRatio×(CurrentEnemyHP%×0.5+0.5)×(1+LevelDifference200)×ThreatLevelDmgModifier


CollisionDamage>=80. If this value goes below 80 for any reason, 80 is
used instead.


There is a small chance for this damage to be dealt twice. The rate for
this to occur is listed below:


EnemySSDMGTwiceChance=0.15×(EnemyEVAEnemyEVA+50)


      Aircraft Crash Damage

This is the damage dealt *by 1 allied plane* reaching the end of the
screen. Allied planes launched in PvE content do not have Aircraft Crash
Damage.


AircraftCrashDmg=(BaseCrashDmg×AVIMultiplier+AlliedShipLevel2)×(CurrentPlaneHP%×0.7+0.3)×(1+LevelDifference200)×TotalDRB×TotalInjureRatio×TotalDRT×EnemyAAModifier

azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Exercise> for more details./


    Slash Damage


      Augment Modules

→ Main article: Augment Module Slash Mechanics <https://
azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Augmentation#Augment_Module_Slash_Mechanics>


      Ship Skills

The general formula for Slash damage is the following:


SlashDamage=A×AverageLevel+B


Where AverageLevel is the average level of all ships with that slash
type. The values of *A* and *B* depend on the attack itself.


    Damage Over Time (DOT)

These are damage sources that deal damage to the target overtime,
without requiring any other weapons / equipment. This damage cannot be
evaded once it is applied.

*Notes*:

  * DoT damage is not affected by any damage dealt / damage reduction
    sources that do not specifically mention "Burn" or "Fire" damage.
  * DoT damage is not added to the end-screen damage scoreboard.
  * StatMultiplier is calculated in the same method as up here
    <#Final_Effective_Stat>.


      Fire / Burn Damage

HE shots each have a probability of starting a fire on the target. If a
fire is started, the ship will be damaged up to 5 times, with the first
instance being 3 seconds after the hit. Damage formula is as follows:

DamagePerTick=(WeaponBaseDamage×WeaponCoeff×SlotEfficiency×StatMultiplier×BurnCoefficient+BurnDamage)×(1+∑inBurnSkillBonusi)

BurnSkillBonus refers to skills that specifically mention "Burn" /
"Fire" damage.


The probability of starting a fire by different types of common guns are
as follows:

Shell Type 	Probability
DD Guns 	1%
CL Guns 	3%
CA Guns 	8%
CB Guns 	4%
Type 3 Ammo 	30%
BB/BC/BM Guns 	50%

There are exceptions to this. Refer to specific equipment pages for
specific information.

Most fires don't stack but they can be replaced or refreshed. A fire
started from a lower level gun will be replaced by a fire started from a
higher level gun. Level increases from DD, CL, CA/CB, to BB guns.
Special barrages may have their own levels. For example, Lexington
class's Artillery Cover applies a Level 1(DD) fire. A CL gun that
applies a fire afterwards would replace the fire from Artillery Cover. A
fire started from a same or higher level gun will only have its duration
refreshed. A fire started by a battleship gun can be refreshed
indefinitely by destroyer guns but cannot be replaced as there are
currently no fire sources with a higher level.


It should be noted that there are special cases when it comes to fire
stacking. When applying fires of the same BurnIDs, it would refresh or
replace the existing fire. Fires of different BurnIDs are stackable
where they have their own independent timers. These unique fires are

      Flood Damage

Several skills can cause flood. Flood damage is caused every 3 seconds,
the first instance at 3 seconds after the effect is inflicted. Multiple
sources of floods can affect a single target at the same time, and will
not interfere with each other, nor with other DoT effects (ex. Burn
Damage <#Fire_/_Burn_Damage>).

DamagePerTick=(WeaponBaseDamage×WeaponCoeff×SlotEfficiency×StatMultiplier×FloodCoefficient+FloodDamage)×(1+∑inFloodSkillBonusi)

    Weapon Accuracy


      Geometric Accuracy

This is the weapon's ability to hit the target *geometrically*. Most
weapons will miss part of their output due to missing the target
outright. Potential reasons include (but are not limited to):

  * Self and/or Enemy ships movement patterns.
  * The weapon's launch pattern.
  * Enemy shields blocking the attack.


      Hit Rate

When a shell/torpedo/bomb *hits a ship geometrically*, there is a chance
that it will be counted as a miss and the projectile won't deal any
damage. HitRate is mainly determined by three factors:

  * The Accuracy Accuracy (Hit) Stat of the shooter.
  * The Evasion Evasion stat of the target.
  * The Luck Luck stat of both ships.

Scaling stats are calculated in the same method as up here
<#Final_Effective_Stat>.

HitRate=0.1+AttackerHitAttackerHit+TargetEvasion+2+AttackerLuck−TargetLuck+LevelDifference1000+AccuracyVsShipTypeSkill−EvasionRateSkill

*Notes:*

  * 0.1<=HitRate<=1.0 . If Evasion Evasion or skills cause the HitRate
    to be below 0.1, then 0.1 is used instead.
  * Evasion EvasionRateSkill are ship skills that directly affect
    Evasion Evasion Rate, such as Smokescreens or Aurora's <https://
    azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Aurora> *The Light of Dawn*.
  * AccuracyVsShipTypeSkill are any ship skills that affect Accuracy
    against a specific hull type, such as Warspite (Retrofit) <https://
    azurlane.koumakan.jp/wiki/Warspite>'s *The Royal Navy Legend*.


      Effective HP

*eHP* stands for Effective Health Pool. It calculates a ship's health
when accounting for enemy accuracy and any damage dealt / resistance
values. For example, if there is a ship that can dodge 50% of the time
and has 2000 HP, another ship that dodges 0% of the time and has 4000 HP
will have the same survivability on average. As always, scaling stats
are calculated in the same method as up here <#Final_Effective_Stat>.


A very simplified eHP formula for an Attacking ship can be derived as
follows:

eHP=[AttackerHPTargetAccuracy×1∏i=1n(1+AttackerDMGResistancei)]


Where AttackerDMGResistance are *negative decimal values* derived from
any damage resistance buffs on the Attacker. This formula gets more
complex the more modifiers you start accounting for, and different
people have different approaches on how to account for everything, hence
why it is not listed on the wiki currently.
